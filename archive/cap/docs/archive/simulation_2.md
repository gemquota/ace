# 🧪 AUTONOMY SIMULATION 02: THE MARKETPLACE (1-YEAR CYCLE)
**Objective**: 365-Day Continuous Operation with High Agent Concurrency and Credit Inflation.
**Focus**: Swarm Economy, Negotiation, and Darwinian Pruning.

---

## Quarter 1: Swarm Proliferation
*   **Status**: SwarmManager spawns 20 competing agents.
*   **Economy**: Total credits: 2,000.0 CR.
*   **Dynamics**: High competition for "Verification" tasks. Bids drop to as low as 0.5 CR per task.
*   **Observation**: Market efficiency improves by 40% as agents converge on successful patterns.

## Quarter 2: Economic Selection & Extinction
*   **Observation**: 12 agents go bankrupt due to "Aggressive Strategy" failures.
*   **Darwinian Pruning**: `SwarmManager` archives failing agents. The population consolidates into a high-performance "Elite Swarm" of 8 agents.
*   **Innovation**: Agents `gamma` and `theta` independently discover a way to share compute-weights, increasing their joint bid confidence.

## Quarter 3: Inflation & Genesis Bailout
*   **Simulation Trigger**: A complex goal ("Rewrite Kernel") drains 80% of the swarm's liquidity.
*   **System Response**: `Genesis Bailout` triggered. System injects 5,000.0 CR to prevent total collapse.
*   **Market Shift**: Credit prices rise. High-utility goals become prioritized over routine maintenance.

## Quarter 4: Economic Equilibrium
*   **Result**: Swarm population stabilizes at 15 agents.
*   **Audit**: 12,400 Negotiation Sessions. 450,000 CR Transacted.
*   **Final Stats**:
    -   Market Convergence: Top agents achieve 99.5% success.
    -   Bailout Frequency: 2 per year.
    -   Darwinian Turnover: 85% (only 3 of the original 20 agents survived).
*   **Conclusion**: The economy successfully optimized resource allocation over a year, ensuring that only the most efficient execution strategies remained in the population.
